SNAP Benefits Fears Among Chinese Elderly Brooklyn Community

- Carol Chen

Chinese elders gathered for a Thanksgiving Turkey Giveaway

At a recent Thanksgiving celebration in Sunset Park, Chinese seniors gathered to share a meal and collect emergency food supplies. But beneath the festive atmosphere, anxiety simmers over looming cuts to the food assistance program many depend on.

The recent federal shutdown offered a preview of what's to come. When SNAP benefits temporarily stopped, over 8,000 Chinese community members served by the Chinese-American Planning Council (CPC) faced immediate food insecurity.

CPC has responded by adding emergency food pantries and pushing state legislators for stopgap measures. Lin emphasized the need for action on multiple fronts: advocacy, policy change, and direct service.

But there's another layer of fear. With increased immigration enforcement in Sunset Park, some seniors worry about accessing help at all. Lin noted community members asking, "What if I go stand on the food pantry line and ICE comes for me?"

Steve Mei, the Community Affairs Director of CPC, said that the organization will restart its biweekly food pantry distribution and educate its senior members about ICE activities.
